HIVE - Understanding Their System

What is HIVE?

HIVE (Hospital In-home Virtual Enabled care) is Australia's first AI-enabled inpatient remote monitoring service. It allows hospital-level patients to be monitored at home while still technically being admitted as inpatients.

How HIVE Works

  • Patient Selection: Clinicians identify stable patients who can be safely monitored remotely (post-surgical, chronic disease management, etc.)
  • Wearable Devices: Patients receive monitoring devices (vitals, SpO2, HR, BP, temperature) that transmit data to the HIVE platform
  • AI Monitoring Layer: Algorithms analyse incoming vitals in real-time, flagging deterioration patterns before they become critical
  • Virtual Ward: Clinical staff monitor a dashboard of all HIVE patients, responding to AI-generated alerts
  • Escalation Protocols: If AI detects concerning patterns, it triggers escalation to clinical staff who can intervene - including bringing the patient back to hospital
  • Integration: Connects to existing hospital EMR/PAS systems for seamless clinical documentation

1. How does Dr. Sophia handle data sovereignty and residency? ▼

Answer: All infrastructure runs on AWS in ap-southeast-2 (Sydney). Patient data never leaves Australian soil. We use AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. Our EKS cluster runs in a dedicated VPC with private subnets. Database (Supabase) is hosted in Australia with row-level security policies.

If pressed on Anthropic/Claude API: "API calls to Claude go to Anthropic's API, but we send only de-identified clinical context - never raw PII. We're evaluating Anthropic's data residency options and AWS Bedrock (which keeps everything in-region) as we scale into government."

2. What's your TGA regulatory position? ▼

Answer: We're currently classified as SaMD (Software as a Medical Device) Class 1 Exempt under TGA guidelines. This is the lowest risk category - we provide clinical decision support and advisory information, not autonomous diagnosis or treatment decisions. A qualified clinician is always in the loop.

If asked about higher classification: "As we move towards more autonomous clinical functions, we have a roadmap to pursue Class IIa classification with ARTG listing. Our architecture is built with this in mind - full audit trail, safety validation at 98%+, and clinical governance frameworks."

3. How do you ensure AI safety in clinical decisions? ▼

Answer: Multiple layers:

  • 42-Agent Consensus Mechanism: Critical decisions go through a Byzantine fault-tolerant multi-agent analysis. Multiple AI agents independently assess the case, and consensus is required before a recommendation is surfaced
  • Safety Middleware: Every AI response passes through content safety validation before reaching the clinician or patient
  • GLP-1 Safety Validator: For our weight loss module, dedicated safety checks ensure medication recommendations follow TGA guidelines (pregnancy contraindications, MTC/MEN2 screening, etc.)
  • Crisis Protocol: Automatic detection of mental health crises with immediate escalation and emergency contact information
  • Clinical Decision Audit: Every AI recommendation is logged with full provenance - what data went in, what came out, and why
  • Human-in-the-loop: Clinician review and approval required for all treatment decisions
4. What encryption and data protection measures are in place? ▼

Answer:

  • At rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data. AWS EBS volumes encrypted. Supabase database encryption enabled
  • In transit: TLS 1.3 for all API communications. ALB terminates SSL with ACM-managed certificates
  • Application layer: Custom encryption module with context-aware encryption (different keys for different data types)
  • Access control: Row-level security (RLS) in Supabase. Role-based access control. API key authentication
  • Secrets management: AWS Secrets Manager for sensitive credentials. No hardcoded secrets in codebase
  • Audit logging: All access and modifications logged with timestamps, user identity, and action details
5. How does your observability and monitoring work? ▼

Answer:

  • Metrics: Prometheus + Thanos for long-term metric storage and cross-cluster querying
  • Logging: Structured logging across all services, aggregated via Loki
  • Dashboards: Grafana dashboards for real-time system health, API latency, error rates, and clinical operation metrics
  • Alerting: Multi-tier alerting strategy - from infrastructure (node health, pod restarts) to application (API errors, consultation failures) to clinical (safety validation failures, adverse events)
  • Tracing: Tempo for distributed tracing across microservices
  • Clinical monitoring: Dedicated dashboards for consultation quality, SOAP note completeness scoring, and safety validation rates

Key stat: "We track diagnostic accuracy at 87-95% and safety validation at 98%+. These aren't aspirational - they're measured continuously."

6. How would Dr. Sophia integrate with existing hospital systems? ▼

Answer:

  • FHIR R4: Full FHIR compliance for EHR integration. We currently integrate with MediRecords via both FHIR and REST APIs
  • Bidirectional sync: Patient data flows both ways - clinical notes, medications, allergies, conditions push to EHR; patient history pulls from EHR
  • HL7 capability: Architecture supports HL7v2 messaging for legacy system integration (common in public hospitals)
  • API-first: RESTful APIs with comprehensive documentation for custom integrations
  • Webhook support: Event-driven architecture for real-time notifications (appointment changes, clinical events)

For EMHS specifically: "We'd work with your integration team to connect via FHIR to your PAS/EMR. The architecture is designed to be EMR-agnostic."

7. What about the DGov AI Self-Assessment? Have you done one? ▼

Answer: We haven't formally completed the WA DGov AI Self-Assessment yet, as we've been operating in the private telehealth space. However, our architecture already addresses the core requirements:

  • Transparency and explainability (full audit trail)
  • Fairness and bias mitigation (multi-agent consensus reduces single-model bias)
  • Privacy and data protection (AES-256, RLS, data minimisation)
  • Accountability (clinical decision logging, human-in-the-loop)
  • Safety and reliability (safety middleware, crisis protocols, adverse event tracking)

"If we move into the WA Health ecosystem, completing the DGov assessment would be one of our first steps. Given our existing governance posture, we're confident it would be straightforward."

8. How do you handle model updates and version control for AI? ▼

Answer:

  • Model versioning: We pin to specific Claude model versions (e.g., claude-sonnet-4-5-20250929) in production. No automatic updates that could change clinical behaviour
  • Staged rollout: All changes go DEV → E2E validation → PR review → PROD. Never direct to production
  • Prompt versioning: Clinical prompts are version-controlled in git with full change history
  • Evaluation pipeline: We use Opik for continuous evaluation of AI response quality against medical benchmarks
  • Rollback capability: Full rollback within minutes if any degradation is detected
9. What's your approach to clinical validation and evidence? ▼

Answer:

  • Evidence-based synthesis: Integrates PubMed research, clinical guidelines, and real-time evidence search (Perplexity AI) to ground recommendations
  • SOAP documentation: Automated SOAP note generation with rubric-based quality scoring ensures clinical documentation meets standards
  • Benchmarking: Tested against MedQA and medical benchmark datasets for diagnostic accuracy
  • Clinical staff oversight: Active clinicians (GPs, nurse practitioners) review and validate AI recommendations in the weight loss module
  • Continuous improvement: Feedback loop from clinicians feeds back into prompt engineering and safety rule updates
10. How does the weight loss / GLP-1 module work specifically? ▼

Answer: End-to-end synchronous telehealth model:

  1. Eligibility screening: BMI check + medical contraindication screening (TGA-mandated hard stops for MTC/MEN2, pregnancy)
  2. AI pre-consultation: Rose (our patient-facing AI) gathers comprehensive medical history, medications, allergies
  3. Clinician video consultation: Booked via Calendly, patient data pre-loaded for the doctor
  4. Prescription management: Through MediRecords integration, including S8 medication tracking
  5. Follow-up pathway: Automated Week 4/8/12 milestone scheduling with AI pre-chat before each follow-up
  6. Safety monitoring: Adverse event tracking, titration safety enforcement, quarterly audit reports

Why this matters to Adam: "This demonstrates a complete, regulated clinical workflow built on our AI platform. The same architecture patterns - safety gates, clinical oversight, audit trails - apply to any specialty."

12. How do you handle adverse events and safety incidents? ▼

Answer:

  • Adverse Event Service: Dedicated service for logging, tracking, and escalating adverse events
  • Immutable audit log: Once recorded, adverse events cannot be modified or deleted (append-only)
  • Automated alerts: Triggers notification to clinical team and practice manager
  • Quarterly audit reports: Automated generation of adverse event summaries for clinical governance review
  • Crisis protocol: Immediate escalation pathway with 5 Australian emergency hotlines (Lifeline, Beyond Blue, etc.)
  • Root cause analysis: Full traceability from adverse event back to the AI interaction that may have contributed
13. What about patient consent and privacy? ▼

Answer:

  • Digital consent: Canvas-based digital signature during onboarding with PDF generation
  • Granular consent: Patients consent to specific data uses (AI consultation, EHR sharing, wearable data)
  • Privacy Act compliance: Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) built into data handling
  • Data minimisation: Only collect and retain what's clinically necessary
  • Right to deletion: Patients can request data deletion (with clinical record retention exceptions)
  • Guest mode: Session-only storage for users who don't want persistent data
14. How does your platform scale? Can it handle public health volumes? ▼

Answer:

  • Kubernetes (EKS): Auto-scaling based on demand. Can horizontally scale backend pods within minutes
  • Karpenter: Node-level auto-scaling for cost-efficient capacity management
  • Current architecture: Handles concurrent consultations with sub-5-second response times for simple queries
  • Cost efficiency: Current EKS infrastructure runs at ~$172/month. Scales linearly with demand
  • Multi-tenant ready: Architecture supports multiple tenants (Botaniqal, Dr. Sophia) on shared infrastructure with isolated data

For EMHS scale (790k+ people): "The architecture is cloud-native and designed to scale. The cost model is primarily driven by AI API calls, which scale proportionally with patient volume. We'd work with your team to right-size the infrastructure."

Answer: Our frontend targets WCAG 2.1 AA compliance:

  • Comprehensive ARIA roles, labels, and live regions throughout
  • Keyboard navigation without traps
  • Screen reader support with descriptive hints (aria-describedby)
  • 4.5:1 colour contrast ratios
  • Voice accessibility: Text-to-speech (ElevenLabs) and speech-to-text for patients who prefer voice interaction
  • Progressive disclosure: Complex medical forms broken into manageable steps

"This is especially important for a public health context where you're serving diverse populations including elderly patients and those with disabilities."
